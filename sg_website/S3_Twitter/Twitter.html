Twitter Analysis


Well, we have collected data and analyzed more than 1000 Spotify genres. But, living in the era of Social Media this was not enough. We were intrigued to investigate how users tweet about music. Is there any specific pattern? Are there differences in how users tweet between different music genres? In order to answer our questions, we downloaded and analyzed more than 2 million tweets. Feel free to indulge in your curiosity and explore our findings!

3. What genre has the happiest Tweets?


To obtain the answer to which genre has the happiest Tweets, we implemented a sentiment analysis. It’s a process of determining whether a piece of writing is positive, negative or neutral. The result, is that, since as you saw above tweets consist mostly of slang and non-legible words, tweets for all genres are more or less neutral. The sentiment score varies from 4.16 - 5.37. Nevertheless, comparing to all the other genres, country, has slightly happiest Tweets compared to other genres.

Final Comment:

2.27 million tweets were downloaded and then ‘cleaned’ to remove gibberish and meaningless tweets. After cleaning we had 2.12 million tweets which were mentioned or hashtag by 2600 distinct artist. We obtained over 25000 distinct words from these tweets, however only 6000 (or 23%) were actual words. Hence, the analysis would be more concrete if we had a larger dataset to execute on. We estimate that we would need at least 25 million tweets to conduct a reliable analysis. So, advice to you people: Make sure to have a very large dataset if you want to do text analysis of tweets.
